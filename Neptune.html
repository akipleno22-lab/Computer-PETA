Planets

    Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and outermost planet in the Solar System, classified as an ice giant due to its composition of hydrogen, helium, and methane—the latter giving the planet its striking deep blue color. It has a solid, rocky core surrounded by a mantle of volatile ices, but no defined solid surface. Neptune is known for its extreme weather, including supersonic winds and massive storms, making it one of the most dynamic planets in the Solar System. It is the fourth-largest by diameter, the third-most massive, and the densest of the giant planets, with a mass 17 times that of Earth. Though slightly smaller than Uranus, Neptune is more massive and compact. It orbits the Sun at a distance of 30.1 AU (4.5 billion km) and completes one orbit every 164.8 Earth years. The planet hosts faint, dusty rings and at least 16 moons, and is symbolized by ♆—Neptune’s trident—reflecting its namesake, the Roman god of the sea.
